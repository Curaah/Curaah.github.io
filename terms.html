Section 01
What Curaah Is (and Is Not)

Curaah is a technology platform that connects patients with hospitals and clinics for OPD appointment booking, queue management, and health record coordination.

Curaah is:

  • A platform for booking OPD appointments at registered hospitals
  • A digital token and queue management system for hospitals
  • A referral coordination system between hospitals
  • A health appointment record for patients

Curaah is not:

  • A hospital, clinic, or any kind of healthcare provider
  • A medical advice service
  • An emergency service — do not use Curaah in a medical emergency
  • A replacement for professional medical consultation
  • An insurance company or claim settlement service
In a medical emergency, call 112 (India emergency) or go directly to the nearest hospital emergency department. Do not use Curaah for emergencies.

Section 04
Appointment Booking

When you book an appointment through Curaah:

  • The appointment is a request made to the hospital — Curaah facilitates the booking but the hospital is responsible for honoring it
  • You must arrive at the hospital during or before your booked time slot — late arrival may result in your token being skipped
  • OPD fees are paid directly to the hospital — Curaah does not collect any fees from patients
  • Curaah is not responsible if a hospital changes doctor schedules, closes unexpectedly, or cannot honor your appointment for reasons beyond our control
  • Always call the hospital directly to confirm before travelling a long distance for an appointment
  • Appointments can be cancelled through your dashboard or by contacting hospital staff directly
Slot availability: Booking a slot does not guarantee that you will be seen at exactly that time. Hospital OPD schedules can vary. The token system gives you a fair queue position — actual waiting time depends on the hospital's patient flow.
Section 05
Token and Queue System
  • Tokens are assigned on a first-come-first-served basis within your booked slot
  • Token numbers reset every day — your token is valid only for the day it was issued
  • Each OPD has its own separate token queue
  • If you do not check in by scanning the QR code or through staff, you will not receive a token and may lose your place in the queue
  • Curaah is not responsible for delays in the hospital's token calling process — queue management is performed by hospital staff
  • Walk-in patients (without Curaah accounts) may be added to the same queue by hospital staff — this is by design to serve all patients fairly
Section 06
Referrals and Proxy Bookings

Referrals:

  • Digital referrals are created by authorized hospital staff on your behalf
  • Referrals include clinical notes written by the referring doctor — these notes become part of your Curaah health record
  • Your verbal consent is required before a referral is created — hospital staff must confirm you agree
  • Curaah does not verify the medical accuracy of referral notes — the referring doctor is responsible for their content

Proxy bookings:

  • Proxy agents are authorized individuals at clinics and dispensaries who can book appointments on your behalf
  • Proxy agents must obtain your verbal consent before making any booking
  • Proxy bookings appear in your Curaah dashboard if you are a registered patient
  • If you believe a proxy booking was made without your consent, contact us at curaahtech@gmail.com immediately
